# Founder Notes

## What This Product Is

WeaR is a wardrobe-first personal styling product.

The central promise is:

- better outfits from the clothes you already own
- better body-aware styling decisions
- more confidence
- less waste

## What This Product Is Not

- not a landing page
- not an admin dashboard
- not an e-commerce storefront
- not a shopping-first recommender

Any future work that pulls the experience toward shopping-first behavior is moving away from the core product.

## Brand Intent

- premium
- youthful
- minimal
- slightly Y2K
- fashion-tech
- confident
- non-corporate

The interface should feel closer to Apple discipline plus luxury editorial polish than generic SaaS.

## Core Product Rules

1. Recommendations must come from the user's own wardrobe.
2. Body and fit intelligence should sharpen the output.
3. AI should feel like a stylist, not a chatbot toy.
4. Shopping can exist later, but only as a secondary layer.
5. The wardrobe should always remain the source of truth.

## UX Rules To Protect

- assistant-first
- low friction
- premium motion
- visual clarity
- fashion-first wording
- no clutter
- no heavy form fatigue

## Current Prototype Strength

The current app already expresses the right emotional direction:

- premium desktop shell
- strong wardrobe-first generation flow
- stylish visual treatment
- upload and review moments that feel product-real

That direction should be protected while the codebase becomes more production-ready.

## Founder-Level Risks

- the app could drift into a generic AI dashboard
- the app could drift into a shopping engine
- the app could lose its premium visual confidence during refactors
- mock content could accidentally be mistaken for product completeness

## What Future Contributors Should Optimize For

- clarity over complexity
- product integrity over feature count
- premium interaction quality over raw density
- believable wardrobe intelligence over flashy AI gimmicks
